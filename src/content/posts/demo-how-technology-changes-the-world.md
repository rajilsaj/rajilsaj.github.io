Technology changes the world less through breakthroughs and more through **access** — taking something that used to require expertise, time, or money, and making it cheap and ordinary.

## Three levers

- **Lowering cost** so more people can participate.
- **Lowering latency** so feedback loops get tighter.
- **Lowering risk** so experimentation becomes safe.

> The best tools disappear. You stop noticing them and just do the thing they used to make hard.

Math renders too, for the curious: $e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$.

That's the lens I bring to side projects — not "what's possible?" but "what's now *easy* that used to be hard?"
